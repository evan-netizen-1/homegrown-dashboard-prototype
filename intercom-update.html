Intercom Onboarding Checklist Update
March 19, 2026

Update the Intercom Onboarding Checklist

Replace the current "Business Information" checklist with a 3-step "Get your first order" checklist focused on activation, not profile completion.

Step 1: Remove These Things

✕
Remove the "Take the Vendor Dashboard Tour" modal
The video/modal that pops up when vendors land on their dashboard. 100% dismissal rate across 42 recorded sessions. Nobody watches it.
✕
Remove or pause the current "Business Information" checklist
The current Intercom checklist tab at the bottom of the dashboard. It asks for business name, profile details, and social media — things that don't lead to the first sale. We're replacing it with a new checklist below.

Step 2: Create the New Checklist

In Intercom, go to Outbound → Checklists → New Checklist and set it up exactly like this:

Checklist name:Get your first order
Audience:All vendors with an active subscription (trial or paid)
Notification style:Snippet (the small non-intrusive tab at the bottom — NOT full-screen)
Show on pages:Any page containing /vendor
Completion celebration:Enable confetti + button "View Your Store"

The 3 Steps (in this exact order)

Step 1: Add a pickup location

Customers can't order without knowing where to pick up.

Step copy:"Tell customers where to pick up. Takes 60 seconds."
Action type:Go to Page URL
URL:/vendor/locations/create
Auto-resolve:When event location_added fires (see Step 3 below for dev work)

Step 2: Add your first product

Just one product to start — they can add more later.

Step copy:"Just a name, category, and price. Photos are optional."
Action type:Go to Page URL
URL:/vendor/products/create
Auto-resolve:When event product_added fires

Step 3: Share your store link

This is how they get their first order.

Step copy:"Text your link to 5 regulars. Most vendors get their first order within 72 hours."
Action type:Go to Page URL
URL:/vendor (dashboard — where the Share button is)
Auto-resolve:When event store_link_shared fires
Note on auto-resolve: The auto-resolve events (location_added, product_added, store_link_shared) need to be sent from our app to Intercom. This requires the dev team to add 3 lines of JavaScript — see "Dev Work Required" below. Until those events are wired up, set the steps to manual completion (vendor clicks to check off) as a temporary fallback.

Step 3: Dev Work Required (for the dev team, not you)

Three Intercom('trackEvent') calls need to be added to the app:

When vendor saves a location:Intercom('trackEvent', 'location_added');
When vendor saves a product:Intercom('trackEvent', 'product_added');
When vendor clicks Share/Copy Link:Intercom('trackEvent', 'store_link_shared');

These go in the existing save handlers for locations, products, and the share button click handler. Each is one line of code.

What About the Business Profile Stuff?

The business name, social media, store description, and other profile steps are not gone — just moved. Create a second checklist:

Checklist name:Make your store stand out
Audience:Vendors who completed "Get your first order" checklist
When to show:After all 3 steps of the first checklist are done

Steps for this second checklist (lower priority — build when you have time):

  • Add a store description
  • Upload a profile photo
  • Add your social media links
  • Add more products (vendors with 5+ get 3x more orders)

Why We're Making This Change

From analyzing 42 Hotjar vendor dashboard recordings:

  • 90% of vendors abandon product creation — the current flow is too long
  • 0 vendors in any recording received an order — they set up but never share their link
  • 100% dismiss the tour modal — nobody watches the video
  • The current checklist asks for profile info — which doesn't lead to sales
  • The #1 thing that produces a first sale is sharing the store link — which isn't in the current onboarding at all

The new checklist focuses on the 3 things required to receive an order: location + product + sharing. Everything else can come later.
